About Our Career Development Program

The Education Institute for Early Intervention (EIEI) Career Development program is designed to empower individuals interested in making a difference in the lives of children with special needs, specifically focusing on early childhood and birth – 8yrs old.

As part of our initiative, we offer a Pennsylvania Department of Labor Registered Apprenticeship in Special Education, a Child Development Associate (CDA) certification, and a Special Education Learn to Earn Program. These opportunities provide hands-on experience, mentorship, and professional training, equipping participants with the skills and knowledge necessary to excel in early intervention and early childhood special education.

Join us to advance your career while making a meaningful impact on children's lives. No prior teaching experience required!

Special Education Paraeducator Apprenticeship

The Education Institute for Early Intervention (EIEI) has launched its Pennsylvania Department of Labor Registered Apprenticeship Program for Special Education Paraeducators, beginning in 2025. This program marks a significant advancement in training the next generation of educators who will play a vital role in supporting students with special needs.

Application Directions

Ready to apply for the 2025 cohort? Follow these steps to submit your application for the Pennsylvania Department of Labor Registered Apprenticeship Program.

Application Steps

  1. Step 1: Verify Eligibility - Confirm you meet all basic requirements (high school diploma/GED, ability to work full-time, background check eligibility)
  2. Step 2: Prepare Documents - Gather required materials including resume, references, and any prior training certificates
  3. Step 3: Complete Application Form - Fill out the official apprenticeship application with personal and employment information
  4. Step 4: Submit Supporting Materials - Include documentation of prior work experience in childcare settings and any unexpired training certificates
  5. Step 5: Interview - Participate in an interview with the EIEI Apprenticeship Coordinator
  6. Step 6: Employer Placement - Once approved, we will help place you with a participating employer
  7. Step 7: Program Start - Begin your 18-month apprenticeship journey!

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Do I need prior teaching experience?

A: No! No prior teaching experience is required. The program is designed for individuals new to the education field. We welcome career changers who are passionate about supporting children with special needs.

Q: How long is the apprenticeship program?

A: The apprenticeship is an 18-month program. During this time, you must maintain full-time employment and complete all required online classes and on-the-job training.

Q: Can I get credit for prior training?

A: Yes! If you have completed required training from another provider, you may be eligible for credit. Submit your unexpired certificates with your application materials for review.

Q: Will I be paid during the apprenticeship?

A: Yes! You "earn while you learn." Apprentices receive paid on-the-job training under the guidance of experienced educators. You will be employed throughout the program.

Q: What if I don't have a job yet?

A: EIEI provides job placement assistance. After your application is approved, we help connect you with a participating employer in our network to begin your apprenticeship.

Q: What credentials do I receive?

A: Upon completion, you receive a nationally recognized credential as a Special Education Paraeducator, official completion certificate from the Pennsylvania Department of Labor, and professional development credits.

Q: What's the cost of the program?

A: Please contact Dr. Essence Allen-Presley at 484-501-9101 or admin@eiforei.org for detailed information about program costs and any financial assistance options available.

Q: What happens if I need to change employers?

A: If your employment is terminated for any reason, contact the Apprenticeship Coordinator immediately. EIEI will assist you in obtaining employment with another participating employer to continue your apprenticeship.
