About The Education Institute for Early Intervention

Empowering childcare professionals and families to create inclusive environments where every child thrives

Our Mission

At The Education Institute for Early Intervention (EIEI), we address a critical issue in childcare—suspensions and expulsions of young children, often due to behavioral challenges linked to inadequate support for children with Individual Family Service Plans (IFSPs) or Individual Education Plans (IEPs).

EIEI educates and empowers childcare professionals to create inclusive environments that foster children's development and well-being. We collaborate with families, educators, and community partners to drive meaningful change, ensuring all children receive the support they need to thrive.

Why EIEI?

The Challenge: Expulsions & Suspensions in Childcare

Current Reality

  • Young children ages 1-5 are suspended and expelled from programs due to behavioral challenges
  • Many full-time and part-time childcare professionals lack training to support children with IFSPs and IEPs
  • Children are never able to return to programs after expulsion

Impact on Children Who Are Expelled:

  • Miss opportunities to grow, develop, and practice social and emotional skills
  • Continue to develop behavior problems that lead to difficulties adjusting to kindergarten
  • Experience harmful effects on development, relationships, education, and health
  • Develop negative views towards school, teachers, and the world around them

Impact on Families with Children with IFSP/IEP:

  • Become frustrated with childcare programs where employees lack training in Special Education Services
  • Experience difficulty with parenting, leading to potential detachment and family risk factors
  • May disassociate from family, friends, and social activities due to child's disability
  • Experience increased stress and financial challenges
  • More likely to seek alternative care, leading to employment instability

Impact on Childcare Staff:

  • Required to manage children with challenging behaviors but often lack education and skills to respond appropriately
  • Low salaries, poor working conditions, and high teacher-to-child ratios
  • Implicit bias leading to stress, burn-out, and low retention rates
  • Limited ability to manage behaviors effectively
