Behavioral Health Services

We recognize that supporting children's behavioral and mental health is integral to their overall development. Our comprehensive behavioral health services are designed to help all students succeed.

Tier 1 - Universal Supports & Services for All Students

Every school has support for all students. All students are taught expected behaviors like how to show respect and kindness. School staff praises children for their excellent behavior.

Examples of services for all students include:

  • Positive Behavioral Intervention & Supports (PBIS) - Promotes positive school safety and good behavior among all students through emotional learning and mindfulness programs.
  • Restorative Justice Programs - Teaches students how to resolve conflicts on their own and in small groups through safe and supportive spaces.
  • Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) - Teaches students how to manage their emotions, make good decisions, and work with other children on a team.

Tier 2 - Individual Supports & Services (30-90 days)

These services identify students with some distress or issues and connect them to resources in or outside the school. Your child receives all Tier 1 supports plus small group or individualized support.

Examples of services may include:

  • Student Assistance Program (SAP) - A team of experts who determine which resources are a good match for your child.
  • Support Team for Education Project (STEP) - Provides behavioral health, counseling, and therapy services plus social services support.
  • Daily behavior reports, behavior contracts, school counseling, check-ins and check-outs

Tier 3 - Intensive Support & Services (60-120 days)

Students with the most distress or needs receive intensive behavioral health services (IBHS). These services aim to help students stay at home and school instead of being placed in a hospital setting.

Examples of IBHS services include:

  • Individual and group sessions
  • Family therapy and case management
  • Behavior management planning
  • Crisis intervention and referrals
  • Outpatient satellite services in schools
24-Hour Mental Health Support: If additional mental health resources are needed, our 24-Hour Mental Health Delegate Line is available at 215-685-6440.
